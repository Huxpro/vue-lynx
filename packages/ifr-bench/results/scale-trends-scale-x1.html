IFR architecture scale trends

Five product architectures on one generated content SFC, scaled from ~1k to ~10k elements. Charts follow the benchmark-playground pattern: log-log polylines plus least-squares scaling exponents α in cost ~ Nα. Source: browser-results-scale-x1.json · 2026-07-17T15:55:52.558Z

FCP vs content scale

Log-log. Same generated SFC at ~1k / 3k / 5k / 10k elements. Lynx for Web, CPU ×1. α ≈ 1 → linear in N; α ≪ 1 → dominated by fixed costs (bundle parse, boot, IPC).

FCP vs main-thread bundle gzip

Same points, x = MT section gzip. IFR copies the app onto the MT bundle; ET adds baked create() code. Moving right without dropping FCP is parse/eval tax without early-paint payoff at that scale.

Scaling exponents

α ≈ 0 → FCP dominated by fixed overhead (bundle parse / boot / IPC). α ≈ 1 → cost grows with content. An architecture that raises MT gzip without lowering α (or FCP) is paying for early-paint capacity that does not show up at that scale on Lynx for Web.
